English Words in the Korean Language
with Slightly Different Meanings

머플러 (muffler)

(muh-peul-uh)

Meaning: scarf
“Nyunyu sells their scarves for cheap” → “Nyunyu가 머플러 싸게 팔아”



패딩 (padding)

(peh-dee-ng)

Meaning: puffer jacket
“Wear a puffer jacket because it’s cold” → “추워서 패딩 입어”



원피스 (one piece)

(won-pee-seuh)

Meaning: dress
“Should I buy this dress?” → “이 원피스 살까?”



이벤트 (event)

(ee-ben-teuh)

Meaning: sale/promotion
“CU is having a BOGO sale” → “CU가 1+1 이벤트 하고 있어”



컨디션 (condition)

(cun-dih-shun)

Meaning: physical condition
“I’m not feeling well” → “컨디션이 안 좋아”



서비스 (service)

(suh-bee-seuh)

Meaning: “free gift”, “on the house”
“I’ll give this to you on the house” → “이거 서비스로 드릴게요”



커닝 (cunning)

(cuh-neeng)

Meaning: cheating (on tests)
“You are not allowed to cheat on this test!” → “커닝하면 안돼요!”



콜 ("call" from gambling terminology)

(koh-l)

Meaning: okay, deal, let’s go

A: "Do you want to go eat tteokbokki?" B: "Yeah, sounds good”
A: "떡볶이 먹으러 갈래?"                B: "콜!”
